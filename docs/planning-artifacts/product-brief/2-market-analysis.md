# 2. Market Analysis

### 2.1 Target Market

**Primary:** Small and medium-sized businesses in Nigeria — specifically online sellers and e-commerce businesses operating on social media platforms.

| Segment | Size |
| --------- | ------ |
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | 5 million online businesses |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | 500,000 active social sellers |
| **SOM** (3-year target, 1% penetration) | 50,000 users |

### 2.2 Target Customer Profiles

##### Primary Segment

- Online sellers on Instagram/Facebook/WhatsApp
- E-commerce store owners managing multiple channels
- Fashion, beauty, food, and lifestyle businesses
- Monthly revenue: ₦100K – ₦350K

##### Secondary Segment

- Digital service providers (consultants, coaches, agencies)
- Local retailers transitioning to digital
- Small marketing agencies managing client accounts

### 2.3 Customer Pain Points

1. Manual social media posting is time-consuming and inconsistent
2. Payment confirmation via WhatsApp screenshots is error-prone and slow
3. No affordable tools combine content creation + customer engagement + payments
4. International tools (Buffer at $15–25/month, Hootsuite) are priced for Western markets
5. Converting personal Instagram accounts to Business accounts (required for API access) is confusing

### 2.4 Competitive Landscape

| Category | Players | Gap |
| ---------- | --------- | ----- |
| **International** | Buffer, Hootsuite, Later | Too expensive ($15–50/month), no Nigerian payment integration, no AI |
| **Local Emerging** | Various | Lack AI integration, single-platform focus, limited features |
| **Our Advantage** | MarketBoss | AI-powered automation at ₦0–₦15,000/month (+ enterprise custom) with local payment integration |
